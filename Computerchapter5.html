Output Devices

Exploring how computers present processed information to us, from digital screens to printed pages.

Soft Copy Output Devices

Soft copy is intangible, digital output viewed on a screen. The main devices are monitors and projectors.

Monitors (VDU)

A monitor, or Visual Display Unit, is a screen that displays output. Key technologies include CRT, LCD, LED, and Plasma.

CRT

Older, bulky monitors using an electron gun.

LCD

Flatter, more efficient, using liquid crystals.

LED

An improved LCD with better contrast and lower power use.

Plasma

Uses ionized gas for high contrast images.

How a CRT Monitor Works

This animation shows how an electron gun fires a beam that scans across a phosphor-coated screen in a zig-zag pattern to create an image, pixel by pixel.

1. Electron Gun: Fires a controlled beam of electrons.

2. Magnetic Yoke: (Not shown) Steers the electron beam with magnetic fields.

3. Scanning: The beam scans across the screen from left-to-right, top-to-bottom.

4. Phosphor Screen: When electrons hit the phosphor dots, they glow, creating a pixel. The rapid scanning creates a complete image.

Hard Copy Output Devices

Hard copy is tangible, physical output, most commonly produced by printers and plotters.

Impact Printers

Create an image by physically striking an ink ribbon against paper. They are generally noisy. Speed is measured in CPS (Characters Per Second) or LPM (Lines Per Minute).

Dot-Matrix Printer

Uses a print head with pins to form characters with dots. It is old technology but can create carbon copies.

Daisy Wheel Printer

Uses a wheel with characters embossed on it. Produces letter-quality text but is slow and cannot print graphics.

Non-Impact Printers

Form an image without striking the paper, making them quieter and faster. Speed is measured in PPM (Pages Per Minute).

Inkjet Printer

Sprays tiny droplets of liquid ink onto paper. Inexpensive to buy, but ink cartridges can be costly.

Laser Printer

Uses a laser beam and toner (powdered ink) for high-quality, fast output. The best quality for personal computers.

Thermal Printer

Uses heat on special thermochromic paper. Commonly used for printing receipts.

Photo Printer

Optimized for printing high-quality photographs with vivid colors.

Dye-Sublimation Printer

Used by graphic designers for high-quality printing on paper and fabric.

All-in-One (Hydra) Printer

A multifunctional device that combines a printer, scanner, copier, and sometimes a fax machine.

Plotters

A specialized device that produces high-quality vector graphics by drawing lines with pens. Ideal for blueprints, maps, and engineering designs.
